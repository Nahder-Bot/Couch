1 · Horizontal · dark · Fraunces italic

The everyday lockup. Use for app store header, website nav, splash screen, social profile cover. Subtle warm halo behind the C adds firelight depth without competing.

couch

Spec
Type: Fraunces, italic, weight 500, opsz 144 (display optical-size). Lowercase.
Color: Wordmark #f5ede1 · Background warm-dark radial #1c1612 → #0d0a08
Spacing: Gap = 28px (≈ ½ icon width). Wordmark vertically centers on icon.
Halo: Radial rgba(217,122,60,0.20) → transparent at 35% radius.

3 · Horizontal · cream

Light-background variant for print, business cards, light marketing. The C-icon's rich shadows still pop on cream because of its dark contrast. Wordmark in deep cognac matches the leather tone.

couch

Spec
Type: Fraunces, italic, weight 500, 84px lowercase.
Color: Wordmark #5b3a22 (deep cognac, matches leather mid-tone) · Background #f1e6d3 (warm cream, NOT pure white)
Spacing: Same as variant 1 (28px gap).
Why cream not white: Pure white kills the warmth and makes the icon look like a sticker. Cream keeps the brand temperature.

4 · Dramatic · Instrument Serif

Cinematic alternative — Instrument Serif italic at large scale. The wordmark dominates more, like a movie title card. Use for hero moments (splash, pitch decks, big marketing). Probably TOO loud for everyday navbar.

couch

Spec
Type: Instrument Serif, italic, weight 400. Lowercase, 110px (40% larger than variant 1).
Color: Wordmark #f5ede1 · Background warm-dark radial.
Spacing: Gap = 36px (more breathing room because wordmark is bigger).
Trade-off: Higher impact, lower versatility. Don't use this in 28px-tall navbars — it would scale poorly. Reserve for marketing and brand "moments."
