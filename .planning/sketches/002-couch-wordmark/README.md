## Design Question

How should the existing 3D-rendered C-sectional icon combine with a typographic wordmark to form a complete brand lockup (logo + name) — without modifying the icon itself, and matching Couch's warm-dark color scheme?

## Constraints (from user)

- **The C-icon must stay exactly the same.** No frame, no overlay, no modification.
- Match the existing brand color scheme (warm dark `#14110f`, cognac browns, cream `#f5ede1`).
- Provide a "nice background" treatment.
- Output usable for both wordmark + full logo lockup contexts.

## Direction

Brand quietness around brand richness. The icon is photographic / 3D-rendered, full of texture and craft. The wordmark should be the OPPOSITE energy — flat, restrained, typographically beautiful — so the two complement instead of competing.

## Variants

- **1: Horizontal · dark · Fraunces italic ★ (recommended default)** — The everyday lockup. C-icon left, "couch" right in Fraunces italic 84px cream. Subtle warm halo behind icon (firelight depth). Use for navbar, splash, social cover.
- **2: Vertical · dark · stacked** — Square contexts (app store badge, social profile, business card center). Same Fraunces, no halo, vertical stack.
- **3: Horizontal · cream background** — Light contexts (print, business cards, email header). C-icon's shadows still pop on cream. Wordmark in deep cognac `#5b3a22`.
- **4: Dramatic · Instrument Serif italic** — Cinematic alternative for hero moments (splash, pitch decks, big marketing). Larger, more dominant wordmark. Trade-off: higher impact, lower versatility — don't use for navbar.

## Universal Rules (across all variants)

- **Don't add anything to the C itself.** Frame it, don't modify it.
- **Lowercase "couch"** — friendly, warm. Title-case ("Couch") feels colder; all-caps ("COUCH") feels harsh.
- **Cream, not white.** Use `#f5ede1` (warm cream) — pure white kills the brand temperature.
- **Generous spacing.** Gap between icon and wordmark = ~½ icon width minimum. Lockup safe area = at least 1× icon height around the lockup.
- **No tagline in the lockup.** "Who's on the couch tonight?" stays in marketing copy, not in the brand mark.

## What to Look For

1. **Default vs Dramatic:** Variant 1 vs Variant 4 — does Fraunces or Instrument Serif feel "more couch" to you?
2. **Halo effect:** Variants 1 and 4 have it; 2 and 3 don't. Is the halo too much, just right, or do you want it on more variants?
3. **Wordmark size:** Variant 1's 84px vs Variant 4's 110px — is the wordmark too dominant in 4, or does that feel right?
4. **Light context (Variant 3):** Does the icon hold up on cream background, or does it look like a sticker/decal?
5. **Vertical (Variant 2):** Useful for app store badge / social profile pic — does the stacked relationship feel grounded?
