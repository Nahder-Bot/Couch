# Phase 11 seed — Teen-mode / Gen Z capture

## Why this isn't in v1

Teen mode is a strategic expansion that requires stable family-mode MVP first. COPPA 2.0 compliance (passed Senate March 2026) adds a scope phase of its own. v1 should ship stable to families before forking.

## Core insight from research

- **53% of teens discuss movies with friends more than social media content**
- **"See a new movie" is #1 weekend activity for 2 years running**
- **Letterboxd owns teen movie TASTE; nobody owns teen friend-group COORDINATION**

Couch's core value prop (decide what to watch together) is MORE relevant to teens than adults — but needs reframing.

## Critical misfit to solve

**Couch's "tonight" cadence assumes 20-min-before planning. Teens plan 2-5 days out with friends.** An "always-on friend room" model fits better.

## Two prototype hooks (research thread 09, ranked by novelty × feasibility)

### Hook 1: Always-On Friend Room / "Couch Channel"
Persistent weekly suggestion room. Friends drop titles throughout the week. Auto-surfaces top-votes by weekend. Like a Discord channel for one concept: what should we watch together soon?

### Hook 2: Watch-Together Streak
Shared streak mechanic for weekly co-watches. Duolingo's Friend Streak delivers +22% DAU lift. Snap streaks reach 90% of US 13-24.

## Parent-branding risk (real but mitigable)

- COPPA 2.0 teen self-consent (13+) removes parental-consent friction for teen accounts
- Distinct teen-mode aesthetic (visual differentiation from family-mode)
- Teen-owned social graph separate from parent's family

## Validation needed BEFORE heavy build

- **5-10 qualitative teen interviews (13-17)** — will they actually adopt a parent-adjacent app with surface separation?
- **Is "weekend friend movie" a strong-enough ritual to hang an app on**, or too situational vs. Discord's always-on utility?
- **Content licensing / filtering UX** for hook "Parent-Unlocked Friday" — how does a parent pre-approve a window without tedious per-title work?

## Estimate

Teen-mode as a phase: ~20-30 hours design + implementation + COPPA compliance + UAT + qualitative validation. Big phase.

## Success criteria

- Teen can sign up without parent involvement (COPPA 2.0 path)
- Teen sees distinct UI from parent-mode family; doesn't feel "my mom's app"
- Weekly friend-room ritual produces at least 1 coordinated co-watch per month at 3-month mark
- Watch-streak visible in teen UI; shareable to Snap/Insta
