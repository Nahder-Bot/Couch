### Task 4 — UAT surfaced to orchestrator (NOT auto-passed)

Per orchestrator directive ("Do NOT mark UAT as PASSED — the orchestrator will track this"), Task 4 is surfaced as `checkpoint:human-verify` rather than auto-completed in this SUMMARY.

The UAT plan body at 15.4-03-PLAN.md Task 4 specifies 4 scripts (UAT 1 Settings UI smoke, UAT 2 push delivery, UAT 3 opt-out, UAT 4 self-echo). Path A two-device test cannot be automated from a single executor session because:
- FCM push delivery requires a real OS-level push subscription on a second physical device.
- The receiver's notification arrival is observable only on the device's lock-screen / notification tray.
- The Settings UI render check requires the user to navigate to Settings → notifications and visually confirm the 9 new toggles + their hint text.

Path A test plan (verbatim from plan body, restated here for orchestrator handoff):

**UAT 1 — Settings UI 9 new toggles (single device):**
- Device A: Settings → notifications. Confirm 9 NEW toggles render with these labels:
  1. "Tonight's pick chosen"
  2. "Couch voted on your pick"
  3. "Your pick was passed on"
  4. "Watch with the couch?"
  5. "Counter-time on your nom"
  6. "Movie starting in 15 min"
  7. "Tonight's pick expiring"
  8. "New episode alerts"
  9. "Couch nudges"
- All 9 default to ON. Hint text matches the locked copy from 15.4-02-PLAN.md (e.g., "Couch nudges" hint = "When someone on your couch wants you on the couch tonight.").

**UAT 2 — F-W-1 push delivery (two devices):**
- Device A signed in as e.g. Nahder. Device B signed in as e.g. Brody (NOT on the couch tonight, but on the same family).
- Device A: Tonight tab → V5 Couch viz → long-press Brody's pill (an OUT pill).
- Expected on Device A: progress underline animation during 700ms hold + flashToast "Push sent to Brody" + haptic feedback.
- Expected on Device B within ~5s: push notification with title "{Nahder's name}" and body "{Nahder's name} wants you on the couch tonight".
- Tap push on Device B: deep-links to /app.
- (Optional) Sentry breadcrumb confirmation: DevTools / Sentry inbox → category='couch-ping' message='fire {brody-id}'.

**UAT 3 — Opt-out verification (two devices):**
- Device B: Settings → notifications → toggle "Couch nudges" OFF.
- Device A: long-press Brody's pill again.
- Expected: Device A still sees the toast (client doesn't know recipient prefs) BUT Device B does NOT receive the push (server-side `sendToMembers` eventType-gate at `queuenight/functions/index.js` reads `notificationPrefs[couchPing] === false` and silently drops the send).
- Device B: toggle "Couch nudges" back ON to leave Settings in default state.

**UAT 4 — Self-echo guard (single device, optional):**
- Device A: long-press OWN pill (the "ME" pill).
- Expected: client-side short-circuit (`memberId === state.me.id → return`) prevents the addDoc write. No push to self.
- Defense-in-depth layers: (1) client short-circuit (saves Firestore RT), (2) rules `senderId == memberId` clause (catches forged client), (3) CF `excludeMemberId` in sendToMembers (catches CF-internal misroute).
