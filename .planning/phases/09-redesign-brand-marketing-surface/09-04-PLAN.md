All of these are direct children of `<body>` today. They inherit the 480px cap from body. To move the cap to .phone-shell, we either:
- Wrap every one individually in `<div class="phone-shell">...</div>`, OR
- Wrap them all in ONE shared `<div class="phone-shell">` that spans from #signin-screen through the end of #app-shell

Recommended: ONE shared wrapper. Fewer DOM nodes, no per-screen wrapping noise, easier revert.

Existing responsive hooks (preserved):
- `@media (max-width:400px){.avatar-grid{grid-template-columns:repeat(5,1fr)}}` — css:1634

- iPad Pro 12.9 portrait: 1024px — yes, this hits the 900px threshold! Intentional per research: iPad Pro in portrait at 1024px benefits from the wider-modal rules. iPad Air 10.9 portrait: 820px — stays on mobile layout (correct).
- iPad in landscape: all models 1194+ — gets desktop layer.

Theme-color meta unchanged; safe-area-inset rules unchanged.

Tabbar clearance invariant: body keeps `padding-bottom: 110px` (the tabbar-height + safe-area-inset-bottom). The .phone-shell wrapper inherits this clearance because body still owns the padding. Do NOT move padding-bottom onto .phone-shell — that would shift the clearance into the centered column and leave the leftover viewport-width margin unclaimed, which looks correct but causes the fixed tabbar to overlay content on narrow viewports due to the transform: translateX(-50%) centering not knowing about the padding-bottom context.
